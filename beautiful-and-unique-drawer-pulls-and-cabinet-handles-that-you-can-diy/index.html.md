# 7 Beautiful and Unique Drawer Pulls and Cabinet Handles That You Can DIY ...

Tired of how your drawers or dresser look? If you have been itching to do some furniture update, something as simple as switching the drawer pulls can be your best move. And because we support all sorts of crafty pursuits, we don't suggest that you buy new drawer pulls. We want you to give the following DIY drawer pulls a try. These are so easy to make and incredibly budget-friendly. Who knows, you might already have the items needed to make the following DIY dresser pulls. Take a look and enjoy your furniture DIY project!

## 1. Toys

_ Got plastic or rubber animal toys forgotten by the little boys in your family? Don't throw them away just yet. You can use them to update the look of your dresser. This project will require hanger bolts, washer and nuts, drill, and gorilla glue. If you want to change the color of the toys, you will need spray paint as well.

Source: apartmenttherapy.com_

## 2. Rock

![Rock](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/cf/gl/ijtsqz215518b1eabac42648604824_600x900.jpg)

That's right, these are rocks with machine bolts attached to one side. Won't they be perfect for a rustic yet semi-industrial look for your home? To attach the bolts to the rocks, you will be needing the assistance of epoxy. Before you start gluing, though, make sure that your rocks are clean. It would help to clean them in soapy water and let them dry for a few days before you start your DIY project.

Source: [lilliedale.blogspot.com](http://lilliedale.blogspot.com/2011/02/sticks-and-stones.html?m=1)

## 3. Paint Brush

![Paint Brush](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/y3/f4/otblw3hc5518b1eb63bd0539828621_427x640.jpg)

This is a great DIY cabinet handle idea for the garage or workshop. For those who do a lot of DIY projects and have a lot of old paintbrushes lying around, this project is right up your alley. Aside from the paintbrushes, this fun project also calls for bolts, washers and nuts, drill, and wrench.

Source: [prettyhandygirl.com](https://prettyhandygirl.com/old-paintbrush-cabinet-door-handles/)

## 4. Copper

![Copper](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c8/le/zl5g0pm95518b1eaed26e618602782_500x685.jpg)

This is gorgeous! I love the look of copper against this delicious shade of blue. For this project, the major materials used were copper pipes, copper bell hangers, copper slip cap fittings, bolts, and nuts, and epoxy. As for tools, you will also need copper cutter and screw driver. For drawers of different sizes, it is best to adjust the length of the copper accordingly.

Source: [upcycledtreasures.com](https://mountainmodernlife.com/)

## 5. Faucet Knobs

_ Looking for a fun nautical furniture redo? There are many fantastic drawer pulls and cabinet handles that come with a sailor and beach theme. They can be quite pricey, though, so we suggest that you go the DIY route. Take some faucet handles, spray paint them in any color you like, and then add nuts and screws. Easy!

Source: witandwhistle.com_

## 6. Leather

![Leather](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/z2/wq/v1qvavve5518b1edb4550221805365_600x855.jpg)

These are beautiful. If you like the look of plain white against gorgeous leather, this is a DIY cabinet handle idea that you will want to consider. Apart from the uniquely chic style, this leather furniture handle idea is also very easy to do. Basically, you will cut a long strip of leather and add eyelets along the two short sides. You use these eyelets to attach the leather to your furniture using screws and washers.

Source: [the-brick-house.com](http://www.the-brick-house.com/2013/02/leather-handle-diy/)

## 7. Branch

![Branch](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/yn/ci/rrnhgwpy5518b1edc6506622555428_306x500.jpg)

This is a fun nature detail that will make your dresser look beautifully unique. There are branch-insired drawer pulls available in stores but they are not easy on the pockets. Why buy when you can just grab a branch from your yard, right? Before you use some as DIY drawer pulls, though, you will want to seal them first with a clear spray. Use more than one layer for a fine finish.

Source: [addicted2decorating.com](https://www.addicted2decorating.com/my-tree-branches-are-in-place.html)

There are boring drawers in my studio screaming for a new look. I will be using some of the ideas above to grant their wish for a makeover. How about you? Which of these DIYs do you fancy?
