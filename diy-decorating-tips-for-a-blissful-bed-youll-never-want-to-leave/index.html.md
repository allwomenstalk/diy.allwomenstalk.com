# 7 Cozy DIY Decorating Tips for a Blissful Bed You’ll Never Want to Leave

Don’t we all dream of having a bed that’s so inviting and blissful that you never want to leave? That dream can become a reality with these practical decorating tips. Whether you're looking to create a **diy cuddle mattress** vibe or just want a peaceful sanctuary, having a blissful bed to climb into at the end of each day helps you instantly relax and makes your sleep sweeter. Soon, it can be your favorite place to spend all your free time.

## 1. Choose a Calming Color Scheme

![Choose a Calming Color Scheme](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/e0/re/s3db2yqp54fcd15e39403568557666_600x450.jpg)

While bright colors are pretty and can make you feel sunny, they aren’t always the best choice for a bedroom. If color doesn’t seem to have an effect on your mood, then this may not be a tip you need. However, most people find certain colors more calming than others. Traditional choices like gray, mint, ivory, or lavender are considered very soothing. If you're looking for something more unique, **teal and purple bedroom** schemes are trending for their sophisticated yet tranquil feel. Exploring **teal and purple room ideas** can help you find the right balance, or you might even consider a **teal pink and purple bedroom** for a slightly more playful, modern aesthetic. Think about how each color makes you feel when you’re making choices for paint and bedding. Explore [DIY projects to personalize your bedroom](https://diy.allwomenstalk.com/diy-projects-to-personalize-your-bedroom/) for more inspiration.

## 2. Make Your Bed Daily

![Make Your Bed Daily](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ke/v9/ko6xbdpf54fcd192eb88a634741638_600x450.jpg)

Making your bed is a small daily chore that makes it so much more inviting. It’s not as pleasant to get into bed when you have to untangle and rearrange covers. Making your bed only takes minutes and is completely worth it when you slip under the covers at night. It’s a habit that may take some time to adjust to, but once you do, you’ll wonder why you haven’t been making your bed all along. For more inspiration, check out these [ways to make your bedroom feel like a hotel suite](https://diy.allwomenstalk.com/ways-to-make-your-bedroom-feel-like-a-hotel-suite/).

## 3. Pile on the Pillows

![Pile on the Pillows](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/j0/ep/z9n7x3ul54fcd1cd7d239776101412_600x379.jpg)

Pillows add an element of coziness to your bed. They make you just want to crawl in and never come out. Purchase some pillows that are comfortable and have textures you like. They’re great for propping yourself up or losing yourself in. Every bed needs a few decorative but usable throw pillows to complete the look. You can find a great variety of high-quality options at [Brooklinen](https://www.brooklinen.com/collections/pillows).

| Material | Feel | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Egyptian Cotton | Crisp & Cool | Hot Sleepers |
| Linen | Textured & Airy | Year-round Comfort |
| Flannel | Soft & Warm | Chilly Nights |

## 4. Add a Throw

![Add a Throw](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/x6/pe/qefpdtin54fcd1e199a8c355897921_600x450.jpg)

It’s always nice to have a throw at the end of your bed. It’s visually appealing as well as an extra layer of warmth you can grab on chilly nights. Take care to choose one that you enjoy the texture of, as you’ll probably find you use only the throw at times. A throw is nice to have when you want to take a quick nap but don’t want to unmake your bed. You can find cozy throws at [IKEA](https://www.ikea.com/us/en/cat/throws-20528/) and catch a few minutes of sleep on top of your made bed.

## 5. Just Add Pets

![Just Add Pets](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/lc/t5/pf9twaxt54fcd2268c660807712359_600x600.jpg)

If you have pets, you might consider allowing them to join you in bed. Having a furry cuddle buddy can make your bed even cozier, especially if you're a single woman. It’s nice to have the company in bed, and it’s also a great time to bond with your pets. You’ll probably find that you look forward to this time together every single day.

### Quick Blissful Bed Essentials

- High-thread-count sheets

- A supportive mattress topper

- A calming room spray or diffuser

- Dimmable bedside lighting

## 6. Don’t Neglect the Nightstand

![Don’t Neglect the Nightstand](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c6/id/ocuulnku54fcd27bb2078150004212_600x463.jpg)

Your nightstand is a big part of having an enjoyable bed that you never want to leave. Make sure your nightstand has all the necessities you need. A lamp is a must. Add in some hand lotion, a bottle of water, and the book you’re reading, and you’re all set. If you don’t have an actual nightstand, you can improvise by using a small table or a shelf. For more tips, see our guide on [how to organize your nightstand](https://diy.allwomenstalk.com/tips-for-organizing-your-nightstand/) for a clutter-free space.

## 7. Add a Fuzzy Rug Nearby

![Add a Fuzzy Rug Nearby](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/w3/kg/d5jkqe7x54fcd28d72e82337376366_600x398.jpg)

This is a special touch you can add when you’re decorating your bed and bedroom. Having a plush rug for your feet to sink into can make your bedroom feel luxurious. To get the one with the best feel, try before you buy. You might feel a little silly, but you’ll be glad you followed this step. Check out some beautiful options at [West Elm](https://www.westelm.com/shop/rugs/all-rugs/). It can be the finishing touch to a truly blissful bed.

These are seven simple things you can do to transform your bed into a blissful sanctuary you'll never want to leave. Whether you start with a new color scheme or simply commit to making your bed every morning, these small changes make a huge difference. Which of these tips do you plan to use first?
