12+ DIY Halloween Headpieces to Make ...

Feathers • Skyline • Toadstool • Zipped • Bat • More ...

By Meream

We have already covered masks but our journey into Halloween crafts is far from over. Today, we check out DIY Halloween Headpieces. If you're not planning on covering your face this Halloween but still want something to wear from the neck above, these DIY headpieces are exactly what you need. Just like ourmask and crown lists, there is something from these DIY Halloween headpieces for all types of costumes and for revelers of all ages.

1. Feathers

Using colorful ribbon, flowers, and feathers, you can make a DIY Halloween headpiece for a native American or perhaps a Boho princess costume. Don't you just love the colors of the ribbons used here? My favorite is the glittery one.

Tutorial

2. Skyline

How cute! As you can see, this is a great Halloween headpiece idea for the family. The basic material used for these headpieces is only corrugated cardboard. You cut it following the top of a building you aim to copy, paint the details, and add elastic for tying around your head.

Tutorial

3. Toadstool

This headpiece idea for Halloween is all sorts of adorable, too. The model here may be a little girl but there is a way to make this perfect for an adult's head. The solution? Use your own hat as base!

Tutorial

4. Zipped

I don't know what sort of costume you can wear with this particular headpiece but it's just too creative and crafty for me not to include here.

Tutorial

5. Bat

I love this! To make this, you will need black paper, craft wire, glue, and a thin headband. You simply cut paper bat shapes, glue them to the craft wire, and wrap the other end of the wire to the headband. Add as many floating bats as you like.

Tutorial

6. Furry Ears

This is my favorite from this list of DIY Halloween headpieces. Why? Because they're cute, easy to make, and I also happen to have all the materials needed to make a pair or two right after I hit publish.

Tutorial

7. Bunny Ears

These bunny ears will probably remind you of the ones worn by fashionistas a few years ago. This pair was inspired by those worn by celebrities, as a matter of fact. Now they're no longer trendy but the spirit of Halloween doesn't care about trends so don't stop yourself from sporting these lacy ears.

Tutorial

8. Medusa

I am not a fan of snakes but I think this headpiece is wonderfully done. The materials you need to recreate this Medusa look are hair extensions, bobby pins, and plastic snakes. If the snakes don't behave, you can always "sew" them to the braided extensions.

Tutorial

9. Rapunzel

Crystal made this yarn hair for her little girl before they watched Tangled. To borrow this idea for Halloween, you will need yarn; lots and lots of yarn. You will also need an old pair of rights and some plastic flowers for embellishing your finished 'do.

Tutorial

10. Rose

This is oh-so-adorable as well. Not only will this be useful for Halloween, I bet you can also customize it for a school play.

Tutorial

11. Bat II

This** DIY Halloween headpiece** idea is also pretty much a bat headband but instead of floating bats, this design comes with a bow-like bat embellishment. I love the fang details.

Tutorial

12. Animal Hats

This last tutorial shows you a way to make five types of headpieces. Check out the pattern after the link to see which size to download and print for your costume.

Frequently Asked Questions

What materials do I need for DIY Halloween headpieces?
Common materials include felt, glue, headbands, fabric, paper, and decorative items like feathers or beads.
Are these headpieces suitable for beginners?
Yes, most DIY headpieces use simple techniques perfect for beginners.
How long does it take to make a headpiece?
On average, each headpiece can take 30 minutes to an hour depending on complexity.
Can I customize the headpieces for my costume?
Absolutely! You can personalize colors and decorations to match your Halloween look.
Are the headpieces comfortable to wear all night?
Most designs prioritize comfort with lightweight materials and adjustable bands.
Do I need any special tools?
Basic tools like scissors, glue guns, and needles are usually sufficient.
Can kids help make these headpieces?
Many projects are kid-friendly but adult supervision is recommended for safety.
Where can I find inspiration for unique headpieces?
Try browsing online tutorials, craft stores, or Halloween magazines for creative ideas.
Is it expensive to make these headpieces?
DIY headpieces are typically budget-friendly, especially using recycled materials.
How do I store the headpieces for reuse?
Store them in boxes or hang them carefully to avoid damage for future Halloweens.
