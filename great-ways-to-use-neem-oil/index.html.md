# 7 Great Ways to Use Neem Oil ...

You may not have heard of it until now, but there are many ways to use neem oil. Neem oil has been used in India dating back to 4,000 BCE, and today, neem oil is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine. Aside from being used medicinally, there are many other ways to use neem oil, which is why you may want to consider keeping some on hand.

## 1. Banish Acne

One of the ways to use neem oil is to combat acne. Neem oil has powerful antibacterial properties that make it great for getting rid of acne. To use neem oil for acne, make a mask by mixing 1 teaspoon of neem oil with 2 tablespoons of French clay and several drops of lavender.

## 2. Eczema Relief

Neem oil can provide relief for symptoms of eczema, which is an inflammatory skin disorder. It helps relieve eczema symptoms because it has both anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. As a result, neem oil quiets the irritation and reduces the pain caused by eczema. For soothing relief of eczema, put some neem oil in your bath water and have a relaxing bath.

## 3. Treat Athlete’s Foot

Athlete’s foot is a fungal infection that no one wants to have. However, if you do happen to get athlete’s foot, it's nice to know that there is a natural remedy like neem oil. Neem oil is a powerful antifungal agent that can combat the fungus that causes athletes foot. To treat athlete’s foot, mix a little bit of neem oil with some coconut oil and apply to the affected area until healed.

## 4. Get Rid of Dandruff

Another way you can use neem oil is to get rid of dandruff, which can be caused by a fungal infection. Using neem oil to treat dandruff is actually very easy. All you have to do is add a 1/2 a teaspoon into your shampoo bottle and shake it to mix the shampoo with the neem oil. Then, you just need to wash your hair! The neem oil won’t get rid of the dandruff immediately, but over time you will notice improvement.

## 5. Natural Mosquito Repellent

If you are trying to avoid harsh chemicals and need a good mosquito repellent, you will be very happy to know that neem oil is a great mosquito repellent. You just need to mix 10 drops of neem oil with ¼ cup of coconut oil. Before heading outside, rub this mixture onto your skin, and you should be mosquito free!

## 6. Non-Toxic Insecticide

Insecticides are extremely toxic, which is why it is best to avoid them. Yet, when you have pests you want to get rid of them as fast as possible. Neem oil can be used as a non-toxic insecticide. You just mix 5 milliliters of neem oil with 1 to 2 milliliters of detergent and 1 liter of warm water. Spray the mixture on all the affected plants until the pests go away.

## 7. Flea and Tick Repellent for Dogs

Because neem oil repels insects very effectively, you can use it as a flea and tick repellent for your dog. By using neem oil to repel fleas and ticks, you won’t have to use toxic flea and tick repellents. The recipe for flea and tick repellent is similar to insecticide. You will need to mix ½ ounce of neem oil with ¼ ounce of mild soap, and 2 cups of water. Spray this mixture onto your dog before heading out into the woods for non-toxic flea and tick repellent.

As you can see, neem oil has many uses. You can use it for everything from acne to mosquitos making it an all-purpose oil to keep in your house. Have you ever use neem oil?
