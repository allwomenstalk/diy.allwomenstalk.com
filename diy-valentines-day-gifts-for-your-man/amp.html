17 DIY Valentines Day Gifts for Your Man ...

Booze Bouquet • Personalized Mug • 52 Things I Love about You Deck of Cards • Love Vouchers • Picture Jar • More ...

By Jenny  · Feb 5, 2026

Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, but don’t worry, you still have plenty of time to make your special guy some of these DIY Valentine’s Day gifts! Everyone enjoys getting gifts from the ones they love. If you make them yourself it gives them an even greater sentimental value. Your man will forever cherish one of these DIY Valentine’s Day gifts because they were handcrafted by you with love.

1. Booze Bouquet

Tutorial

When your guy hands you a bouquet of flowers this Valentine’s Day, you can blow his mind by giving him this easy to make booze bouquet! This is one of the easiest to make DIY Valentine’s Day gifts, anyone can make this and it takes very little time. You can substitute candy bars if your guy isn’t much of a drinker.

2. Personalized Mug

Tutorial

This is one of the cheapest DIY Valentine’s Day gift you can make, but it may also be the sweetest. You can buy plain white mugs from most dollar stores and personalize them with any phrase, lyrics or quotes that symbolize your love for your sweetie. Write anything you would like on your mug. My husband and I got married last February and our first dance song was "Lucky" by Jason Mraz and Colbie Caillat, so I wrote a lyric to the song on a mug for him. He absolutely loved it! It makes him think about our wedding every time he uses the mug.

3. 52 Things I Love about You Deck of Cards

Tutorial

This is such a sweet and meaningful idea! It costs hardly any money to make but it is absolutely priceless! All you need is a deck of cards, a Sharpie, a hole punch, and binding rings. On each of the 52 cards, write one thing you love about your guy. Decorate the top card any way you’d like. You can also write on paper and use Mod Podge to adhere the paper to the cards if you’d prefer.

Many gifts have become popular among couples. It’s time for us to start looking for more niche gifts. You can personally design cufflinks for your man.

Men generally have their own shirts. If they want to be more refined, men usually choose to use cufflinks as decorations to make themselves more elegant. At this time, the custom cufflinks you gave him will come in handy. Every time he sees the cufflinks, he will think of your thoughtful design. Of course, cufflinks can also be collected as gifts, which are memorable no matter what time!

4. Love Vouchers

Tutorial

Love Vouchers are a classic Valentine’s Day gift. Several stores sell them around this time of year, but you can actually make your own (for FREE)! They are cute and simple and a great way to add some spice to your relationship! After you have printed out the amount of coupons that you would like, simply fill out the coupons with whatever romantic, loving or naughty things you would like to offer to your love.

5. Picture Jar

Tutorial

This is such a darling idea! I would love to have several of these displayed throughout my home! It is a very unique idea and perfect for showing off pictures of the two of you as a couple.

6. Love in a Jar

Tutorial

This DIY Valentine’s Day gift is so adorable and sweet! Your significant other will love this to pieces! It is the personal and loving gifts like this that make Valentine’s Day so special.

7. Ceramic Photo Coasters

Tutorial

You can make these gorgeous coasters for any occasion, but they are especially meaningful on Valentine’s Day. To personalize these you could use photos of you as a couple, pictures from places you have been (either your own or from postcards) or anything else that is meaningful to the two of you.

8. Nip with Love

Tutorial

These will surely be a hit with your boy! They're cute, they're small, and they're great if you're looking for something pocket-sized or even something that he can travel with. Just tie a little cute note around his favorite nip and he'll think you are the bee's knees!

9. Sports Fan?

Tutorial

Do you have a man who is a huge baseball fan? Well, why not create something that he will absolutely love and always remember? Scribble down a few of your favorite phrases or even just detail out why you're in love with him -- on a baseball!

10. Sugary Sweets

Tutorial

While you probably don't think that gift baskets are the way to go, what about one like this? Get together quite a few of his favorite candies and write adorable notes on them! This is the alternative mentioned earlier, if your honey doesn't like booze.

11. Things I Want to do with You Booklet

Tutorial

So we've talked about love vouchers and things you love about your man, but what about what you want to do with him? Well, that's a gift in and of itself! Make a booklet, a little different than the former one but in the same spirit -- and with the same tutorial!

Frequently Asked Questions

What are some easy DIY Valentine's Day gifts I can make for my guy?
Oh, there are so many sweet things you can craft! Think about a jar filled with little love notes, a handmade photo album, or baking his favorite cookies. Guys love heartfelt stuff just as much as we do!
I'm not super crafty. Any simple DIY gift ideas that won't turn into a disaster?
Absolutely, no worries! You can go for something like a playlist of songs that mean something to both of you. Or grab a deck of cards and write one thing you love about him on each card. Simple, yet super thoughtful!
Are DIY gifts really appreciated by guys on Valentine's Day?
For sure! It's the thought and effort that count. A DIY gift shows you took the time to make something special just for him. That alone can make any homemade gift way better than something store-bought.
I want to make something unique for Valentine's Day. Any out-of-the-box ideas?
Get creative with a DIY coupon book of things he loves—like back rubs or a home-cooked meal. Or if he's into games, create a 'love scavenger hunt' with little surprises and messages.
How much time should I set aside to make a DIY Valentine's gift?
It really depends on the project. Some can be whipped up in an hour, like a batch of heart-shaped brownies. Others, like a knitted scarf, might take a few evenings. Just remember, it's all about enjoying the process and putting your love into it!
