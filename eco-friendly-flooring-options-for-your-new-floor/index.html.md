# 7 Eco\-Friendly Flooring Options for Your New Floor ...

If you are replacing the floors or carpet in your house as a part of your redecorating project, you should consider looking at some eco-friendly flooring options. Eco-friendly flooring is much better for your health and the environment than some of the common flooring used in many homes. Laminate wood planks contain formaldehyde, vinyl planks contain PVC, and many carpets contain Teflon. These chemicals can be emitted into the air for years after the floor is installed and cause health problems such as asthma. Thankfully, there are much better alternatives, and it is relatively easy to find eco-friendly flooring options.

## 1. Cork

![Cork](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/js/zi/myot4aig_600x578.jpg)

Cork flooring is gaining popularity among people who want eco-friendly flooring. It is made from the cork oak tree, which doesn’t have to be cut down to harvest the bark. This makes cork a very sustainable option for floors. Aside from being sustainable, cork is also naturally fire retardant, and it naturally repels bugs and microbes. These qualities make cork a wonderful choice when it comes to eco-friendly flooring options.

## 2. Bamboo

![Bamboo](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/sx/wa/sebagqfx_600x903.jpg)

Because it grows so fast, bamboo is considered to be sustainable. While it is a sustainable resource, you do want to be careful when buying bamboo flooring and make sure the stain used on the floor is either non-toxic or has a low VOC rating, which means it doesn’t emit many chemicals.

## 3. Linoleum

![Linoleum](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hw/y4/hu7mprqx_600x800.jpg)

Linoleum has been around since before the 1940s, and it is a surprising eco-friendly flooring option. Linoleum is made from linseed oil, cork dust, tree resins, wood flour, ground pigments, and ground limestone, which means you don’t have to worry about it emitting harmful chemicals. Linoleum is extremely durable and comes in a variety of patterns, making it great for kitchens and entryways.

## 4. Concrete

![Concrete](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ro/ro/c8ycr2zr_600x769.jpg)

Concrete is probably the last thing you would think about when thinking about indoor flooring. However, polished concrete can be made to look like wood and marble. It is amazing. Obviously, concrete is extremely durable, but you do want to carefully consider what rooms you use it in. It is not the best option for a TV room because it can echo.

## 5. P.E.T. Berber Carpet

![P.E.T. Berber Carpet](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hv/dr/b28fdtm8_600x769.jpg)

P.E.T. Berber carpet is a polyester carpet that is made from recycled plastic bottles. Making P.E.T. carpet has very little impact on the environment, which is why it is a great eco-friendly option. P.E.T. Berber carpet is durable, spill-resistant, and economical, which is why it is a wonderful option for living rooms, family rooms, and bedrooms.

## 6. Reclaimed Hardwood

![Reclaimed Hardwood](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/s1/ev/lclmtgs2_600x902.jpg)

Hardwood floors are generally not considered very eco-friendly. However, if you want real wood floors, reclaimed hardwood is a great option. Reclaimed hardwood is wood that has been reclaimed from old buildings. It is more expensive than some of the other flooring options, but it does look wonderful.

## 7. Glass Tiles

![Glass Tiles](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ii/kw/z11duy7q_490x640.jpg)

Glass tiles are made from recycled wine and beer bottles, which make them a fantastic option for an environmentally sustainable floor. Glass tiles are perfect in a bathroom because they do not absorb moisture, so there is no worry about mold growth. One of the great things about using glass tiles is there are many different color options and patterns that you can choose. Additionally, they reflect light and make a small space seem very bright.

As you can see, there are many options when it comes to eco-friendly flooring. You are not limited to just a few choices. You can choose from many different types of flooring so you can have the perfect floor for your home. Which eco-friendly flooring option would you use?
