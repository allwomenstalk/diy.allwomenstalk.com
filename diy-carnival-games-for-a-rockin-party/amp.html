3 Ring Toss

Via Trains, Thomas the Tank, Carnival ...

I think plastic bottles weighted with sand or rice would be a better choice than glass, especially if kids will be playing.

***

Ring toss is a classic carnival game that is easy to set up and fun to play. The goal of the game is to toss a ring around a bottle to win a prize. To play, you will need a set of plastic rings, weighted with sand or rice, and several plastic bottles. The bottles should be of different sizes and heights to increase the difficulty of the game. Place the bottles in a row, and mark a line a few feet away from the bottles. Players stand behind the line and take turns tossing the rings. The player who gets the most rings around the bottles is the winner.

To make the game more exciting, you can offer different prizes for each bottle. For example, the smallest bottle could be worth a small prize like a toy or candy, while the tallest bottle could be worth a bigger prize like a stuffed animal. You can also offer bonus points for rings that land on top of the bottle.

8 Mini Stilts

Fun for kids and adults (sober or not).

***

Mini stilts are a great addition to any carnival-themed party. Not only are they fun for kids, but adults can also join in on the fun. Mini stilts are easy to make with just a few basic supplies, such as wood, screws, and rope. To make your own mini stilts, start by cutting two pieces of wood into the same size and shape. Next, drill two holes into each piece of wood, making sure they are lined up exactly. Then, thread a piece of rope through each hole and tie a knot at the end. Finally, attach the two pieces of wood together with screws.

Once your mini stilts are assembled, you can have your guests start walking around on them. It’s a great way to get people laughing and having fun. You can even have a mini stilt race, where your guests can compete against each other to see who can make it to the finish line first.
