# The Simplest Way to Bathe Your Dog: 7 Stress\-Free Steps

Bathing your dog is never going to be fun. After all, who likes the smell of wet dog? That doesn’t mean you should keep putting it off, though. A clean dog smells better, is less itchy, and is so fluffy and cute you won’t be able to resist a snuggle. If you are looking for the [easiest way to bathe a dog](#6), you probably want to get it over with quickly. Check out these simple steps for bathing your dog, and you’ll be ready to snuggle in no time.

| Step | Action | Key Benefit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Prep & Tub | Reduces anxiety for the dog |
| 2 | Wetting | Ensures deep cleaning |
| 3 | Lathering | Removes dirt and odors |
| 4 | Rinsing | Prevents skin irritation |
| 5 | Drying | Keeps your pet comfortable |
| 6 | Grooming | Prevents tangles and mats |
| 7 | Rewarding | Builds positive associations |

## 1. Put Your Dog in the Tub

![Put Your Dog in the Tub](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/n8/w7/0r/jc8bs895_600x400.jpg)

It’s probably much easier to get your dog into the tub if it isn’t already full of water, which definitely makes bathing your dog simpler. That way you can pet and calm him while you run the water. If your dog is deathly afraid of the running water, try filling it first and see if you can coax him in. In my experience, however, it’s easier for everyone if you turn the water on after you have the dog in the tub. Use a [non-slip mat](https://www.akc.org/expert-advice/vets-corner/how-to-bathe-a-dog/) to help them feel more secure.

## 2. Pour Water on the Dog

![Pour Water on the Dog](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c0/jy/n5/nxca7sls_600x398.jpg)

Use a cup or other container to soak your dog with water. Keep in mind that water tends to bead on dog fur, so you’ll probably need to rub him down as you pour the water so you have enough to suds him up with shampoo when the time comes. Make sure you lift his head and wet down his neck and toss some water at his belly too. This is one of the essential [dog bath steps](#2) to ensure the coat is fully saturated.

## 3. Soap Him down

![Soap Him down](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wt/kx/zi/rlprbcz3_600x431.jpg)

Unless your dog loves a bath, you’ll want to move pretty quickly here or you’re bound to be battling your pet back into the tub or you’ll be covered in water that he just shook off. Squirt some [dog-specific shampoo](https://www.chewy.com/b/shampoo-conditioner-413) into your hand and start rubbing it gently into his fur and skin. Start at his ears and move down, making sure you get paws, belly, and neck as you go. Keep the soap out of his face so you don’t get it in his eyes.

## 4. Rinse

![Rinse](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/zv/dv/ll/n1p1oh27_600x458.jpg)

Turn the faucet back on and use warm water to rinse your dog’s fur. Rub him as you go to be sure you’re getting all the suds. Any that gets left behind can make him pretty itchy, and you won’t want to start all over if that happens. Use a flat hand to “wring” your dog out by pressing lightly to remove some of the water. Continue rinsing until the water runs off clear and there isn’t any soap left behind. Proper [dogs bathing techniques](#4) focus heavily on a thorough rinse.

## 5. Towel Dry

![Towel Dry](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/li/0f/oq/qxcko06i_600x448.jpg)

I strongly suggest using old towels for this job. The bigger the better! Use a towel to gently rub your dog dry. You won’t be able to get all the water out and he’ll still be a bit damp, but make sure you get as much as possible so he doesn’t get cold. Let him run around a bit to finish getting dry. If you've ever wondered "[why does my dog rub his face on my towel](https://www.thesprucepets.com/why-do-dogs-zoom-after-a-bath-4589252)?" it's usually just a way to dry off and get their scent back.

## 6. Comb

![Comb](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fd/ar/ox/dz8stgcz_600x398.jpg)

Use a specially designed dog comb to lightly brush your dog’s fur. Get out any clumps and crud left behind after the bath. I like to do this outside if it’s warm enough so that I don’t have the added chore of also cleaning up all the fur off the floor. You can also have your dog stand on a towel while you comb him and then shake off the fur out in the yard before washing the towel.

## 7. Treat

![Treat](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ro/e1/h3/exc4v0jj_600x398.jpg)

You want your dog to behave during a bath, so giving him a treat is a great way to do this. Granted, it might take a few trips to the tub before he starts to connect his bath with his treat, but once you get him there, getting him into the tub is much easier. These [steps of bathing](#1) should always end on a high note.
