# Accessories to Make Your Dorm Room More Homey ...

There are many accessories for your dorm room that you'll notice people buying at the beginning of every school year, but there are just a few that will make your room much more inviting. If you feel like your dorm room looks more like a jail cell than the homey room you were hoping for when you got to college, try some of these **accessories for your dorm room**.

## 1. Posters

![bed frame, duvet cover, furniture, bed sheet, bed,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ah/vk/fkp3rkbb5b8c29d9a7d9c460932965_600x600.jpg)

One of the classic accessories for your dorm room is posters. There are so many pretty posters that can cover up your bare dorm walls and give your room a homier look. Posters are much cheaper than regular artwork but can look just as nice if you find the right ones. Many campuses have a poster sale at the beginning of the year, so this is a great place to find what you’re looking for!

## 2. Lamps

![furniture, room, shelf, product, wall,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/xe/hk/wzqsmsxx5b8c29fce60be982936621_600x600.jpg)

Lamps are probably the most inviting accessory you can add to your room. Overhead lights that are typical in dorm rooms often give a strange blue tinge to the room, but lamps can make the atmosphere so much more comfortable. Investing in a couple of lamps can make your dorm room feel more like your bedroom at home, and thus so much more inviting.

## 3. Mirrors

![plant, leaf, flowerpot, houseplant, vase,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/cy/x6/o7mu0od45b8c2b8d9a7e1713975429_600x600.jpg)

Dorm rooms are known for being small, and mirrors can give the illusion of more space. If you feel claustrophobic in your room, try placing some mirrors around the walls and see it open up!

## 4. Rug

![room, bed frame, bed sheet, bedroom, furniture,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/gm/a5/qqdtixai5b8c2a1eaa116289337881_600x600.jpg)

Dorm rooms can appear very cold when you are first move in, due to the bare floors. Rugs make the room so much warmer and welcoming. (Not only that, but they muffle the sound of your roommate's heels when she's coming in at two in the morning.) There are many pretty rugs out there, so find one that matches your room’s colour scheme.

## 5. Pictures

![room, cushion, bedroom, furniture, bed sheet,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/gj/o4/thsrseki5b8c2abb367ce608856715_600x750.jpg)

Almost everyone I know has pictures of their family and friends around their desks or beds. These are nice touches to a dorm room and are often a conversation starter when you have friends over. There are also so many ways to organise your photos around your room, so this is an accessory you can have fun with! Find a new or unusual way to display them.

## 6. Fairy Lights

![room, bedroom, wall, bed, furniture,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/k5/n5/srud04f45b8c2b14b1023168411275_600x600.jpg)

When I first went to college, I saw that people used fairy lights to decorate their rooms. I absolutely love this trend! You can have different colour lights for different months (orange for October (Halloween), coloured for December (Christmas), red for February (Valentine’s) etc.), but you can leave white lights up school year round. Fairy lights create a nice atmosphere in your room, and they look pretty around windows and mirrors.

## 7. Fish Bowl

![tableware, glass,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ia/cd/wyeebpzg5b8c2bb429a23687292253_600x750.jpg)

If you want to add a really homey touch to your dorm room, try adding a fish! Fish are so easy to take care of and can look so pretty on an empty shelf in your dorm room. (I could never find an empty shelf!)
