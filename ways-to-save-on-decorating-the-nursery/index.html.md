# 7 Ways to save on Decorating the Nursery ...

Discovering the ways to save on decorating the nursery can be very beneficial. Baby items are expensive and definitely add up, so saving where you can helps immensely! Of course, some items are worth the price, like car seats and chemical-free products, but most items, including decor, can be purchased on the cheap. Here is a list of great ways to save on decorating the nursery!

## 1. Make a List of Needs

The first thing you should do when thinking about the ways to save on decorating the nursery is to make a list! Categorize the list into furniture, decor and essential baby items that you know you're going to need right away. Being organized in this way will allow you to be less overwhelmed with all that you need and you can clearly see the areas of saving as well as splurging.

## 2. Prioritize Items

Along the same lines as making a list, you should prioritize what you need most and right away after baby arrives. Many items can be purchased at a later time, such as a crib, since most parents choose to keep their baby with them in a bassinet for the first few months.

## 3. Buy Used when You Can

Consignment shops, thrift stores, garage sales and even your own friends may have items you can buy that are of great quality and are less expensive! Often times baby items, including furniture, aren't well-used and can look new or simply be in need of a fresh coat of paint. Start searching around and you may find treasures that will save you money!

## 4. Be Crafty!

Sometimes the best way to decorate is to find things in your own home and use your crafting skills to come up with creative and cute decor items! Some options are knitting a baby blanket, putting together a handmade mobile and finding magazine cut-outs for artistic and cute wall-hangings! The options are endless (and mostly inexpensive!) if you decide to be crafty.

## 5. Skip on the Bedding Sets

Crib bedding sets are expensive and come with loads of bulky items that are not only unnecessary, but dangerous. Baby's shouldn't sleep with bulky blankets, pillows or soft bumpers. These all pose a greater risk for SIDS, so the best option is to buy a few fitted crib sheets and a sleep sack/swaddling blanket for your little one!

## 6. Repurpose Household Items

Do you have an old dresser that you don't use anymore? A great option is to repurpose and recycle it by using it for your nursery! Paint it a cute color, replace the knobs and you're set! You can even find thrifty deals on furniture that can be transformed to fit your nursery.

## 7. Buy Multi-purpose Furniture

Another great option that saves you money is to buy items that have multiple functions. A convertible crib of good quality will last your child years because it can eventually transition to a full-sized bed. A changing table can double as a dresser or desk, and thus can be converted once the child is out of diapers.

Planning your baby's nursery is one of the most exciting things when it comes to preparing for his/her arrival! It can also be stressful when you're on a budget, but with a little planning and searching around, you can save yourself some cash! Would you care to share any other ideas about saving money when decorating a nursery?
